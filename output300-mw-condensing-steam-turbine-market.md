Recombustion turbines utilize a secondary combustion process to increase overall efficiency and power output by reintroducing exhaust gases. In contrast, non-recombustion turbines operate solely on the initial steam generated, focusing on direct conversion of thermal energy without additional combustion steps. Each type serves distinct operational needs and efficiency goals, catering to varying power generation requirements in large-scale applications.</p></p>
