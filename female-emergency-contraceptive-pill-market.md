<p><p>The female emergency contraceptive pill market primarily features two types: Levonorgestrel and Ulipristal Acetate. Levonorgestrel is a progestin that prevents ovulation if taken within 72 hours after unprotected intercourse, while Ulipristal Acetate is a selective progesterone receptor modulator effective up to 120 hours post-intercourse, delaying ovulation and altering uterine lining. Both options are essential for reproductive health, providing women with choices for preventing unintended pregnancies, influencing market dynamics based on accessibility, awareness, and regulatory factors.</p></p>
