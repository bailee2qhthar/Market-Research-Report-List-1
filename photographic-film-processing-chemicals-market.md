<p><p>Photographic film processing chemicals encompass various substances essential for developing photographic images. Sodium thiosulfate acts as a fixer, removing unexposed silver halides. Sodium hydroxide serves as a developer, aiding in the reduction of silver halides to metallic silver. Acetic acid is used as a stop bath to halt the developing process. Potassium alum functions as a hardening agent, enhancing film durability. Potassium bromide acts as a restrainer to control development speed. The "Others" category includes additional specialty chemicals used in niche applications.</p></p>
